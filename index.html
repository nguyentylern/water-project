Water

Water is an important nutrient to human health; it helps your body remain stable and remove waste[1]. Looking towards the future to preserve human health, water must remain. As a result, conserving water is important because it allows for its continued use. Conservation also has other effects: by using less water, it can save money, help the environment, and lower energy demand[2].

Additionally, water treatment is important because it allows for the safe consumption of water. Water treatment is a highly involved process, utilizing chemicals like chlorine, iron, and salts and other materials like gravel and filter membranes[3]. Without treatment, water might be contaminated with objects, dust, bacteria, and viruses that can be harmful when consumed or can cause disease.
